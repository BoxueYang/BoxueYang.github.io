    Tech Report

    Innovator-VL: A Multimodal Large Language Model for Scientific Discovery
    Boxue Yang (Project Lead & First Author), et al.
    [GitHub] [Project Page]

    • Led the development of a Multimodal LLM designed for science, open-sourcing both Instruct and Thinking versions.
    • Built a fully transparent, end-to-end reproducible training pipeline, achieving SOTA with less than 5 million high-quality scientific data samples.
    • Introduced the GSPO algorithm for the Thinking model, significantly improving inference efficiency. The project has received 100+ Stars.
    ICML 2026 (Under Review)

    EvoStreaming: Your Offline Video Model Is a Natively Streaming Assistant
    Boxue Yang (First Author), et al.

    • Proposed a new paradigm to activate streaming video understanding capabilities without modifying the model architecture.
    • Constructed the first evaluation framework (RealStreamEval) that models streaming video understanding as frame-level multi-turn dialogue.
    • Designed a zero-human-annotation self-evolution pipeline (EvoStreaming), achieving a 10.8% performance boost on OVOBench with only 1k data.
    Tech Report

    AI for Service: Proactive Assistance with AI Glasses
    Boxue Yang (Core Contributor & First Author), et al.

    • Proposed the "AI for Service" paradigm and built a proactive assistance system based on egocentric video streams.
    • Designed the Alpha-Service framework and an edge-side multi-agent collaborative architecture for resource-constrained AI glasses.
    • Realized the shift from "Human finding Service" to "AI finding Service," applied in scenarios like blackjack consulting and museum guiding.
    ICML 2026 (Under Review)

    Robust Graph Representation Learning via Adaptive Spectral Contrast
    Zhuolong Li, Boxue Yang, Haopeng Chen

    • Pioneered a spectral adversarial training framework for graph contrastive learning, optimizing objectives via a minimax game.
    • Designed dual-channel adaptive spectral filters and a novel Projected Gradient Attack (PGD), improving average classification accuracy by 2.82% on 9 benchmarks.
    • Significantly enhanced model robustness against poisoning attacks and noise perturbations.
